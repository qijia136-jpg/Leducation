Who This Retreat Is For

10-Day Journey Framework

Use this as the customer-facing structure until the team confirms the final dated itinerary.

Day 1

Arrival and Orientation

Airport or city arrival support where available, welcome briefing, safety notes, schedule overview, and rest after travel.

Days 2-3

Culture and Gentle Rhythm

Guided cultural visits, tea or local craft learning, neighborhood walks, and light wellness activities suited to a restorative travel rhythm.

Days 4-6

Immersive Wellness Practices

Slow-paced sessions such as mindful movement, breathing, food culture, traditional lifestyle learning, and reflective time.

Days 7-9

Local Life and Signature Experiences

Deeper cultural immersion, hosted activities, scenic or heritage visits, group sharing, and optional personal time when available.

Day 10

Departure and Follow-Up

Departure coordination, feedback collection, and support for onward travel questions.

Zhejiang and Shanghai Elements

These elements can make the retreat feel less like a generic trip and more like a place-based wellness journey. Final route, opening status, and hosting arrangements should be confirmed by operations.

Zhejiang

山水文化与静养节奏

Zhejiang can anchor the retreat through mountain scenery, tea culture, garden-style stays, quiet walking, local food, and a calmer pace that supports mind-body restoration.

  • Use as cultural learning, not a religious or medical promise.
  • Prepare simple context notes before arrival.
  • Check route, transport, walking intensity, and weather plan before confirming.
Shanghai

抵达、转换与城市缓冲

Shanghai can serve as a convenient arrival, departure, or transition point, helping guests move from international travel into a slower Zhejiang retreat rhythm.

  • Useful for flight coordination and first-night adjustment.
  • Good for a light city culture or food module.
  • Avoid overloading the schedule before the retreat begins.
Experience Design

Wellness Without Overclaiming

Position the program as restorative travel: slower mornings, guided walks, tea, food culture, journaling, breath awareness, and gentle movement where suitable.

  • No medical diagnosis or treatment language.
  • Escalate health and mobility needs to human support.
  • Use "wellness-oriented" instead of "therapeutic cure".

Candidate Stay Concepts

The following stay styles can be used for product planning. Treat all lodging as candidate elements until the team verifies address, availability, room standards, group capacity, meal support, and cancellation terms.

Candidate stay

花园民宿型住宿

Use as a garden-style accommodation concept: quiet courtyards, slower pacing, tea or conversation spaces, and a softer first impression for travelers.

Operations check
  • Room count and private bathroom standards
  • Breakfast, dinner, and dietary accommodation
  • Vehicle access, luggage handling, and nearby walking conditions
Candidate stay

山居静养型住宿

Use as a mountain-retreat lodging concept: calm air, scenery, reading time, light movement, and post-activity rest after cultural visits.

Operations check
  • Altitude, road access, stairs, and rain-day plan
  • Heating/cooling, sound insulation, and bedding quality
  • Group gathering space for briefing or sharing
Candidate stay

文化度假型住宿

Use as a culture-forward lodging concept: landscape, local life, quiet design, and retreat-friendly gathering space can support the wellness journey if verified by the team.

Operations check
  • Whether the property branding and service match overseas guests
  • English or bilingual support availability
  • Photos, room categories, invoice/payment process, and group policy
Internal note: do not publish prices, exact hotel commitment, or room promises until the supplier confirms in writing. The public QA can mention these as "candidate stay styles" and route booking-specific questions to email.
