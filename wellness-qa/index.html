10-Day Premium Journey

A high-end retreat rhythm: morning practice, curated cultural experience, recovery time, and a light evening ritual. Final dates and suppliers remain subject to confirmation.

Day 1

Arrival, Private Check-In

Shanghai or Zhejiang arrival support, welcome tea, retreat orientation, preference review, and quiet evening recovery.

Day 2

Meditation and Calligraphy

Morning breathwork and seated meditation, followed by calligraphy practice, tea, journaling, and a relaxed welcome dinner.

Days 3-4

West Lake and Ink Painting

West Lake-inspired slow walk, Chinese ink painting, garden lunch, optional massage or rest block, and evening reflection.

Days 5-7

Porcelain, Water Town, Brewery

Porcelain making and painting, China water-town ancient streets, boutique brewery visit, craft beer tasting, and gentle social evening.

Days 8-10

Integration and Departure

Quiet movement, optional cultural excursion, closing circle, personal consultation, departure coordination, and follow-up support.

Zhejiang and Shanghai Elements

These elements make the retreat feel like a place-based wellness journey rather than a generic tour. Final route, opening status, and hosting arrangements should be confirmed by operations.

Zhejiang

Zhejiang Slow Culture

Zhejiang can anchor the retreat through West Lake ambience, water-town scenery, tea culture, porcelain craft, garden-style stays, quiet walking, local food, and a calmer pace that supports mind-body restoration.

  • Use as cultural learning, not a religious or medical promise.
  • Prepare simple context notes before arrival.
  • Check route, transport, walking intensity, and weather plan before confirming.
Shanghai

Shanghai Arrival and Transition

Shanghai can serve as a convenient arrival, departure, or transition point, helping guests move from international travel into a slower Zhejiang retreat rhythm.

  • Useful for flight coordination and first-night adjustment.
  • Good for a light city culture, dining, or gallery module.
  • Avoid overloading the schedule before the retreat begins.
Experience Design

Wellness Without Overclaiming

Position the program as restorative travel: slower mornings, guided walks, tea, meditation, calligraphy, ink painting, porcelain craft, journaling, and gentle movement where suitable.

  • No medical diagnosis or treatment language.
  • Escalate health and mobility needs to human support.
  • Use "wellness-oriented" instead of "therapeutic cure".

Candidate Stay Concepts

The following stay styles can be used for product planning. Treat all lodging as candidate elements until the team verifies address, availability, room standards, group capacity, meal support, and cancellation terms.

Candidate stay

Garden Retreat Stay

Use as a garden-style accommodation concept: quiet courtyards, slower pacing, tea or conversation spaces, and a softer first impression for travelers.

Operations check
  • Room count and private bathroom standards
  • Breakfast, dinner, and dietary accommodation
  • Vehicle access, luggage handling, and nearby walking conditions
Candidate stay

Quiet Nature Stay

Use as a nature-retreat lodging concept: calm air, scenery, reading time, light movement, and post-activity rest after cultural visits.

Operations check
  • Road access, stairs, and rain-day plan
  • Heating/cooling, sound insulation, and bedding quality
  • Group gathering space for briefing or sharing
Candidate stay

Cultural Boutique Stay

Use as a culture-forward lodging concept: landscape, local life, quiet design, and retreat-friendly gathering space can support the wellness journey if verified by the team.

Operations check
  • Whether the property branding and service match overseas guests
  • English or bilingual support availability
  • Photos, room categories, invoice/payment process, and group policy
Internal note: do not publish prices, exact hotel commitment, or room promises until the supplier confirms in writing. The public QA can mention these as "candidate stay styles" and route booking-specific questions to email.
