A. About This Trip

QA1. What exactly is this program?

This is a 10-day China cultural immersion and high-end customized wellness journey, not a standard sightseeing tour. The itinerary combines Shanghai's cosmopolitan energy, China Jingdezhen's porcelain heritage, Zhejiang wellness practices, Taoist cultural context, calligraphy, ink painting, Jiangnan water towns, West Lake aesthetics, a brewery experience, and refined dining throughout.

QA2. Is this a medical retreat or clinical treatment?

No. This is a culture and wellness-oriented journey. It does not provide medical care, clinical treatment, nursing, or guaranteed health outcomes. Clients requiring medical support should contact human support before booking to confirm suitability.

QA3. Who is this trip designed for?

It is ideal for international guests seeking a slower, deeper, more comfortable way to experience China: couples, friends, small families, private small groups, cultural enthusiasts, and light-luxury travelers. It is especially suited for those interested in meditation, calligraphy, ink painting, porcelain, Taoist culture, and refined Chinese dining.

QA4. Who should confirm suitability first?

Clients requiring medical care, clinical treatment, nursing care, full wheelchair accessibility, or zero tolerance for weather or supplier adjustments should contact human support first. This is a cultural and wellness journey, not a medical service.

QA5. Is this suitable for children?

The journey is designed mainly for guests who are comfortable with slower cultural workshops, quiet practice, and premium travel pacing. Families with children age 12+ may be considered case by case. Please contact human support with the child's age and travel experience for confirmation.

QA6. What language is the tour conducted in?

The journey is conducted in English by default. Chinese-speaking guides or staff will also be present. If other languages are needed, please inquire in advance because availability and potential surcharges depend on the specific dates and locations.

QA7. Is this a group tour? Will I be joined with strangers?

No. This product is positioned as high-end private customization. It usually operates as a private group for a family, couple, or invited party. If a client is open to a specific small-group date, the team will separately confirm group size, configuration, pricing, and service standards.

Refund, cancellation, and date-change policies must be confirmed through the formal quotation or contract. Rules vary by hotel, restaurant, transport provider, workshop vendor, and holiday period.

Pricing defaults to standard twin-share occupancy. Single occupancy, room upgrades, suites, connecting rooms, or special-view rooms require a supplement and are subject to hotel confirmation.

QC2. What is the hotel standard?

Accommodation is planned around five-star luxury hotels or near-five-star standard properties. Specific hotels are confirmed based on route, dates, room availability, group size, and final quotation. Do not commit to a specific hotel name or room type before formal confirmation.

QC3. Can I choose my own hotel?

Yes, but they must be declared at booking. Vegetarian, seafood, nut, dairy, alcohol-free, halal, religious, and allergy-related needs require advance vendor confirmation.

D. Itinerary and Logistics

QD1. What does the 10-day route look like?

Core route: Shanghai arrival, China Jingdezhen porcelain experience, Zhejiang wellness retreat, Jiangnan water towns, West Lake, and return logistics. With international flights crossing time zones, the full journey is commonly communicated as 11 days and 9 nights.

QD2. Why is Jingdezhen early in the itinerary?

Porcelain-making requires subsequent finishing, kiln firing, and protective packing. Placing Jingdezhen early maximizes the chance for clients to take home completed porcelain pieces by the journey's end.

QD3. What can clients take home?

Depending on workshop schedules and completion timelines, clients can take home their own porcelain pieces, calligraphy, and ink painting works. Porcelain involves firing and packing, so the final take-home format depends on the studio's actual completion status.

QD4. How is transportation arranged?

Private car service and chauffeur-driven ground transport are arranged for confirmed city and intercity travel. High-speed rail with business-class seats is arranged where the route requires it. Specific transfer times and connections are confirmed in the final itinerary.

QD5. Is airport pickup and drop-off included?

Private airport pickup at Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG) and departure drop-off are included for confirmed arrangements. For arrivals at Hongqiao (SHA) or other airports, please specify at booking so arrangements can be adjusted.

QD6. What is the typical daily schedule like?

The pace is slower than standard group tours. A typical day may include morning wellness or cultural activity, late-morning excursion or workshop, refined lunch, afternoon exploration or free time, and evening dining. There is built-in rest and flexibility, but clients should still expect walking, transfers, workshops, and intercity movement.

E. Wellness and Activities

QE1. What does the wellness retreat include?

It can include seated meditation, breathwork, gentle body practice, tea, writing, calligraphy, ink painting, and elements connected to Tiantai Mountain and Tongbai Palace Taoist cultural context. Use phrases like cultural understanding and body-mind relaxation; avoid promising therapeutic or clinical outcomes.

QE2. Is prior meditation or art experience required?

No prior experience is required. Workshops are designed for beginners and led by experienced instructors in a gentle, exploratory, culturally immersive way.

QE3. What is the connection to Taoist culture?

The Zhejiang wellness segment draws on the cultural context of Tiantai Mountain and Tongbai Palace, an important site in Chinese Taoism. The practices are presented as cultural and wellness heritage, not religious instruction. Participation is cultural and voluntary.

QE4. Is alcohol served? Is the brewery experience required?

The brewery visit and tasting is a relaxed social and contemporary-China lifestyle component. Clients who do not drink alcohol may opt out or request non-alcoholic alternatives. The brewery segment can also be replaced with an extended tea experience if requested during customization.

QE5. What is the difference between Jiangnan water towns and West Lake?

Jiangnan water towns emphasize canals, stone bridges, alleyways, courtyards, and waterside daily life. West Lake emphasizes lake vistas, bridges, classical gardens, layered mountain silhouettes, and Chinese landscape aesthetics.

F. Health and Accessibility

QF1. How physically demanding is this trip?

The pace is gentler than standard tours, but there will still be walking, getting in and out of vehicles, site visits, workshops, and intercity transfers. Clients with knee, back, cardiac, respiratory, sleep, chronic-condition, or mobility concerns should disclose them in advance.

QF2. Is this trip wheelchair accessible?

This journey is not designed for full wheelchair accessibility. Some heritage sites, water-town lanes, and temple areas have steps, uneven surfaces, and narrow passages. Clients with mobility aids should contact human support for a case-by-case assessment before booking.

QF3. What if I have a medical emergency during the trip?

The guide and team will assist in arranging transport to the nearest appropriate medical facility. Clients should carry their own travel insurance with medical coverage. The journey does not provide on-site medical personnel or clinical care.

G. Travel and Entry Requirements

QG1. Which airport should I fly into, and when should I book flights?

The recommended arrival airport is Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG). The specific arrival and departure city is confirmed based on the client's origin, flight pricing, travel dates, route, and final hotel arrangements.

China-US round-trip flights are not included. After the itinerary is confirmed, clients should book China round-trip flights as soon as possible to reduce the risk of limited seat availability, sharply rising fares, or related losses.

QG2. Who handles visas and entry documents?

Clients are responsible for confirming China entry requirements based on their passport and departure country. The team only assists by providing the itinerary schedule and invitation letter when appropriate. The team cannot guarantee visa outcomes, and entry policies may change, so clients should always verify official sources.

If confirmed booking information is needed for visa materials, clients should understand that visa refusal may still create non-refundable costs or other losses that must be borne by the client according to the booking terms.

QG3. What should I pack?

Recommended: comfortable walking shoes, lightweight layers, seasonal outerwear, rain protection, sun protection, personal toiletries and medications, modest clothing for temple or meditation settings, and a small daypack for workshops. Detailed seasonal packing suggestions are provided in the pre-trip briefing.

QG4. Is WiFi available throughout the trip?

Hotels provide WiFi, and private vehicles may have mobile WiFi. Some international apps and services may be restricted in China without a VPN. Clients should install and test any needed VPN before departure.

QG5. Can I use credit cards in China?

International credit cards are accepted at major hotels and some upscale restaurants, but not universally. WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate daily transactions. Clients are advised to set up Alipay or WeChat Pay with an international card before travel and carry some Chinese Yuan cash for small vendors.

QH1. Is this trip fully private and customizable?

Yes. The journey is designed as high-end private customization with private car service. Clients may express preferences for pace, dining, room type, cultural experiences, shopping, rest time, and more. The team adjusts based on feasibility.

QH2. Can the itinerary be changed after booking?

Yes, customization requests are welcome. The team confirms adjustments based on date, transport, hotel, restaurant, workshop, and vendor availability. High-end private itineraries are generally more flexible, but changes may affect final price.

QH3. Can I add extra days before or after the tour?

Yes. Pre-tour and post-tour extensions can be arranged, such as extra Shanghai days or side trips to Suzhou, Nanjing, Huangshan, Beijing, Xi'an, Guilin/Yangshuo, or Chengdu. Additional hotel nights, transport, and activities are quoted separately.

QH4. What if the weather is bad?

Photos and route descriptions are for reference only. Due to weather, vendor availability, traffic, and on-the-ground conditions, activity sequence, visit points, and timing may be adjusted. Actual arrangements on the day take priority.
