10-Day Flow

A Premium Retreat Rhythm

Inspired by high-end retreat design, the itinerary balances morning practice, curated cultural immersion, rest windows, and light evening integration. Final routing is confirmed after guest preferences, season, and supplier availability are reviewed.

Day 1
Arrival and Private Check-In
Arrive in Shanghai or Zhejiang. Airport greeting and private transfer can be arranged. Check in, enjoy welcome tea, review personal preferences, and recover quietly from international travel.
Day 2
Meditation and Calligraphy
Begin with breathwork and seated meditation. A calligraphy mentor introduces brush, ink, posture, and rhythm, turning Chinese characters into a meditative practice. Afternoon tea and journaling help guests settle into the retreat pace.
Day 3
West Lake Ambience and Ink Painting
Enjoy a slow scenic walk inspired by West Lake aesthetics. In the studio session, guests learn how ink, water, empty space, and brush pressure create classical Chinese landscape mood.
Days 4-5
Porcelain Making and Painting
Work with clay and porcelain forms, then paint porcelain surfaces with guided motifs. This is designed as a tactile cultural experience rather than a technical class, suitable for first-time guests.
Days 6-7
Water Town and Craft Brewery
Walk through canal lanes, stone bridges, courtyards, and old-town textures. Later, visit a boutique brewery to understand contemporary craft culture in China, with relaxed tasting and non-alcoholic alternatives on request.
Days 8-9
Integration and Elegant Rest
Morning meditation, light movement, personal time, optional cultural deepening, and a closing circle.
Day 10
Departure and Follow-Up
Departure coordination, feedback collection, and support for onward travel questions.

Daily Detail

Visits and Experiences

The descriptions below are written for client-facing use. They explain why each visit matters, what guests do on site, and how the day feels.

Day 1

Arrival, Welcome Tea, and Retreat Orientation

Guests are met at the airport or rail station after flight confirmation. The first evening is intentionally soft: check-in, welcome tea, a short orientation, and time to unpack. The team reconfirms dietary needs, walking comfort, and next-day timing.

Meals: welcome tea / light dinner subject to arrival time. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 2

Seated Meditation and Calligraphy

The morning introduces breath, posture, and quiet attention. In the calligraphy session, guests learn the relationship between brush pressure, ink flow, body alignment, and rhythm. The focus is not perfect handwriting; it is entering Chinese culture through movement and presence.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 3

West Lake Ambience and Chinese Ink Painting

A slow scenic excursion introduces the classical mood of water, bridges, gardens, and layered views. The afternoon ink painting workshop translates that atmosphere into brushwork, empty space, tonal gradation, and mountain-water composition.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 4

Porcelain Forms and Clay Practice

Guests visit a porcelain studio or craft space and learn how clay becomes vessel, surface, and memory. The session is designed for first-time makers, with guided forming, object observation, and cultural context around Chinese ceramics.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 5

Porcelain Painting, Tea, and Reflection

Guests paint porcelain using accessible motifs inspired by landscape, floral forms, or personal symbols. The day slows down with tea and journaling, giving everyone time to connect the handmade object with the broader retreat theme.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.

Daily Detail

Scenery, Craft, and Contemporary Taste

The second half of the program adds contrast: water-town atmosphere, modern craft culture, spacious rest, and a thoughtful close.

Day 6

Water Town Ancient Lanes and Canal Walk

Guests explore a Chinese water-town setting with canals, stone bridges, courtyards, local textures, and small shops. The visit is paced for observation and photography rather than rushed sightseeing.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 7

Boutique Brewery Visit and Craft Beer Tasting

A guided brewery visit introduces contemporary Chinese craft culture: ingredients, fermentation, production flow, and tasting language. Non-alcoholic alternatives can be arranged so the activity remains inclusive and relaxed.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, tasting/light dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 8

Gentle Movement and Cultural Deepening

Morning meditation and light movement help the group integrate the travel rhythm. The afternoon can be used for a deeper art session, tea, spa-style rest, or a light local visit depending on guest energy.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 9

Integration, Closing Circle, and Farewell Dinner

The final full day is calm and ceremonial. Guests review handmade works, share reflections, enjoy personal time, and gather for a farewell dinner that closes the retreat without rushing the emotional transition.

Meals: breakfast, lunch, farewell dinner. Stay: premium hotel or boutique retreat stay.
Day 10-11

Departure Transfer and International Return

The team assists with check-out, transfer, rail or airport timing, luggage movement, and onward travel questions. For long-haul flights, arrival home may be the following calendar day.

Meals: breakfast on Day 10 where hotel timing allows. Day 11 is an international flight buffer.
