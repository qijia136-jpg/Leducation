10-Day Flow

A Premium Retreat Rhythm

Inspired by high-end retreat design, the itinerary balances arrival ease, curated cultural immersion, several days of wellness practice, rest windows, and light evening integration. Final routing is confirmed after guest preferences, season, weather, and supplier availability are reviewed.

Day 1
Shanghai Arrival, Bund and City Welcome
Arrive in the international metropolis of Shanghai. Check in, rest, then begin with a refined Bund and city introduction that helps guests transition into China with comfort and style.
Day 2
China Jingdezhen, the Porcelain Capital
Transfer to China Jingdezhen for porcelain shaping, painting, and kiln handoff. Starting porcelain early allows time for finishing, firing, and travel packing before departure.

Daily Detail

Visits and Experiences

The descriptions below are written for client-facing use. They explain why each visit matters, what guests do on site, and how the day feels.

Day 1

Shanghai Arrival, Bund Walk and City Welcome

Guests arrive in Shanghai, check in, and begin the journey with a composed city introduction: the Bund, contemporary Shanghai energy, and a gentle welcome that sets a premium international tone before moving into deeper Chinese cultural immersion.

Meals: welcome tea / light dinner subject to arrival time. Stay: five-star luxury or near-five-star hotel in Shanghai.
Day 2

China Jingdezhen, the Porcelain Capital

Guests transfer to China Jingdezhen, the Porcelain Capital, for a guided studio session. They work with clay, vessel proportion, hand pressure, and accessible blue-and-white painting motifs. Selected works are handed to the studio for firing, finishing, and protective packing.

Meals: breakfast, light-luxury lunch, light-luxury dinner. Stay: five-star luxury or near-five-star hotel in or near Jingdezhen.

Daily Detail

Practice, Brushwork and Landscape

After the Jingdezhen studio work enters the firing process, the journey moves into Zhejiang for Taoist-inspired practice, calligraphy, ink painting, and quiet integration.

Day 3

Zhejiang Arrival and Tongbai-Inspired Wellness Practice

Guests arrive in Zhejiang and enter a quieter Jiangnan setting. The day introduces breath, posture, seated meditation, tea, and Taoist cultural context connected to Tongbai Palace traditions, helping the group shift from travel into a steady retreat rhythm.

Meals: breakfast, light-luxury lunch, light-luxury dinner. Stay: five-star luxury or near-five-star hotel.
Day 4

Calligraphy Meditation and Chinese Ink Painting

A calligraphy mentor introduces brush pressure, ink flow, body alignment, and rhythm, turning Chinese characters into a meditative practice. The ink painting session translates stillness into brushwork, empty space, tonal gradation, and mountain-water composition.

Meals: breakfast, light-luxury lunch, light-luxury dinner. Stay: five-star luxury or near-five-star hotel.

Practice and Contemporary Taste

The Zhejiang retreat section keeps a slower wellness rhythm, then adds a half-day brewery experience for contrast and social ease.

Day 5

Gentle Movement and Taoist Cultural Deepening

Morning meditation and light movement help the group integrate the travel rhythm. The afternoon can deepen Taoist cultural learning, tea practice, journaling, or an additional art session depending on guest energy.

Boutique Brewery Visit and Craft Beer Tasting

A guided brewery visit introduces contemporary Chinese craft culture: ingredients, fermentation, production flow, and tasting language. Non-alcoholic alternatives can be arranged so the activity remains inclusive and relaxed.
